› About

Hey, I'm Eduardo.

ML & systems engineer based in Portugal — I build production AI and I'm happiest near the metal, where the abstractions get thin.

I'm a software engineer currently building core infrastructure for Germany's largest digital tenant-protection platform — async AWS Lambda pipelines and the event-driven architecture behind them. Most of my day-to-day is production backend and ML work, but the parts I enjoy most are the ones where you stop trusting the abstraction and have to understand what's actually happening underneath.

Before this I spent two years shipping production AI — RAG pipelines, LLM-powered chatbots, and NLP classifiers, including a university chatbot deployed on-premises. On my own time I write a fair amount of low-level C++: a Vulkan renderer and path tracer built from scratch, where the problems are device selection, command buffers, memory, and synchronization rather than frameworks.

Lately I've been drawn to where ML meets embedded systems — running quantized neural networks on bare-metal microcontrollers, and a side project modelling spiking neural networks for EEG motor-imagery. The long-term pull is neuroengineering, but honestly the systems problems are worth solving on their own.

◆ What I work on

Production ML systems and the backends around them. GPU and rendering code in modern C++. And increasingly, machine learning on constrained hardware — quantization, TinyML, and the embedded toolchain.
